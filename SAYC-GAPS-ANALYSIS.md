### 14. Defensive Leads and Signals (CRITICAL GAP)

**What we have:** Nothing. The convention card has no section on defensive play.

**What's missing (from SAYC reference):**

- AKx(x): lead A

- QJx(x): lead Q
- JT9(x): lead J

- AKJxx+: lead A

**Impact:** Critical. Defensive leads and signals are a standard part of any convention card. The ACBL convention card form has dedicated sections for leads and carding agreements. This is arguably the biggest gap in the document.

### 18. Slam Bidding Beyond Blackwood (IMPORTANT GAP)

**What we have:** Blackwood (4NT), Gerber (4♣), DOPI.

**What's missing:**

**Grand Slam Force (5NT):**
A jump to 5NT (not preceded by 4NT) asks partner to bid 7 with 2 of top 3 trump honors (A, K, Q).

**Cuebidding:**
After trump agreement, bidding a new suit below game shows a control (ace or void, then king or singleton) and slam interest. This is the standard method for investigating slam without committing to Blackwood.

**ROPI (Redouble = 0, Pass = 1):**
Used when opponent doubles the Blackwood response (as opposed to DOPI which is used against overcalls).

**Impact:** Important. Cuebidding and GSF are standard SAYC tools for slam investigation. Without them, the only slam tool available is Blackwood/Gerber, which is insufficient for many slam hands.
