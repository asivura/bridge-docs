# Precision Club System

An artificial "Big Club" system where 1♣ shows all strong hands (16+ HCP), leaving other openings limited and well-defined.

## System Overview

| Feature | Precision Specification |
|---------|------------------------|
| 1♣ Opening | **16+ HCP, artificial** |
| 1♦ Opening | 11-15 HCP, catch-all |
| 1♥/1♠ Opening | **11-15 HCP, 5+ cards** |
| 1NT Opening | **13-15 HCP** |
| Strong Opening | 1♣ (not 2♣) |
| Origin | Taiwan (C.C. Wei, 1969) |
| Popularity | Asia, International |

### Core Philosophy

The cornerstone is the **limited 1♥/1♠ opening** (11-15 HCP). Responder immediately knows opener's range, making subsequent bidding more precise.

## Opening Bids

| Open | HCP | Hand Description |
|------|-----|------------------|
| **1♣(!)** | **16+** | Artificial, any distribution |
| 1♦(!) | 11-15 | 2+♦, no 5-card major (catch-all) |
| 1♥ | 11-15 | **5+♥** |
| 1♠ | 11-15 | **5+♠** |
| 1NT | 13-15 | Balanced, no 5-card major |
| 2♣ | 11-15 | 6+♣, or 5♣ with 4-card major |
| 2♦(!) | Special | Multi (weak major) OR 22-23 balanced OR 4441 |
| 2♥ | 6-11 | 5+♥ and 5+ minor |
| 2♠ | 6-11 | 5+♠ and 5+ minor |
| 2NT | 6-11 | 5-5 in minors (weak or strong) |
| 3♣/3♦ | 8+ | 7+ semi-solid suit |
| 3NT | - | Gambling (solid 7+ minor) |

### 1♦ "Catch-All" Opening

The 1♦ opening handles all 11-15 HCP hands that cannot open:
- 1♥/1♠ (needs 5-card major)
- 1NT (needs balanced 13-15)
- 2♣ (needs 6+ clubs)

May be opened with as few as **2 diamonds** with 4-4-3-2 shape.

## Responses to 1♣

The **most important structure** in Precision. 1♣ is forcing; responder cannot pass.

### Negative Response

| Response | HCP | Meaning |
|----------|-----|---------|
| **1♦(!)** | 0-7 | Negative, any shape |

### Positive Responses (8+ HCP, Game-Forcing)

| Response | HCP | Meaning |
|----------|-----|---------|
| 1♥ | 8+ | 5+♥, game forcing |
| 1♠ | 8+ | 5+♠, game forcing |
| 1NT | 8-13 | Balanced, no 5-card suit |
| 2♣ | 8+ | 5+♣, game forcing |
| 2♦ | 8+ | 5+♦, game forcing |
| 2♥ | 4-7 | 6+♥, constructive (not GF) |
| 2♠ | 4-7 | 6+♠, constructive (not GF) |
| 2NT | 14+ | Balanced, slam interest |

| 2NT | 19-21 | Balanced |

## Two-Level Openings

### 2♣ Opening (11-15 HCP)

Shows **6+♣** or **5♣ with 4-card major**.

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 2♦(!) | Asking bid (relay) |
| 2♥/2♠ | Natural, to play |
| 2NT | Invitational |
| 3♣ | To play |

### 2♦ Multi Opening

Shows one of three hand types:
1. Weak two in ♥ (6-11 HCP, 6+ hearts)
2. Weak two in ♠ (6-11 HCP, 6+ spades)
3. 22-23 HCP balanced
4. 4-4-4-1 with 16+ HCP

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 2♥(!) | Pass or correct (assuming weak major) |
| 2♠ | To play if spades, else opener bids |
| 2NT(!) | Asking - strong inquiry |
| 3♣/3♦ | Natural, to play |

### 2♥/2♠ Openings (Two-Suiter)

| Opening | Shows |
|---------|-------|
| 2♥ | 5+♥ and 5+ minor, 6-11 HCP |
| 2♠ | 5+♠ and 5+ minor, 6-11 HCP |

### 2NT Opening (Minors)

Shows **5-5 in minors**, either:
- Weak (6-11 HCP), or
- Strong (fewer than 5 losers)

## Asking Bids

A key feature of Precision are **asking bids** for slam exploration.

### Trump Asking Bid (TAB)

A **direct raise** of partner's suit asks about trump quality.

| Steps | Holding |
|-------|---------|
| 1st step | No top honor (A/K/Q) |
| 2nd step | One top honor, 3 cards |
| 3rd step | One top honor, 4+ cards |
| 4th step | Two top honors, 3 cards |
| 5th step | Two top honors, 4+ cards |
| 6th step | A-K-Q |

### Control Asking Bid (CAB)

After TAB, bidding a new suit asks about controls in that suit.

| Steps | Holding |
|-------|---------|
| 1st step | No control (xxx+) |
| 2nd step | Third-round control (Qx or doubleton) |
| 3rd step | Second-round control (Kx or singleton) |
| 4th step | First-round control (Ace or void) |
| 5th step | AK or AQ |

## Key Conventions

### Impossible Negative

After 1♣ - 1♦ - 1♥/1♠, responder's jump to 3 of opener's suit shows 4-card support with the 1♦ negative (0-4 HCP with fit, or 5-7 with good fit).

### Unusual Positive

A jump response to 1♣ (e.g., 1♣ - 2♠) shows a semi-solid 6+ card suit with limited values (4-7 HCP).

### RKCB (1430)

Roman Key Card Blackwood for slam bidding:
- 5♣ = 1 or 4 key cards
- 5♦ = 0 or 3 key cards
- 5♥ = 2 key cards without trump Q
- 5♠ = 2 key cards with trump Q

### Interference Defense

Against opponent's double or overcall of 1♣:
- **Pass** = 0-4 HCP (trap or weak)
- **Redouble** = 5-7 HCP
- **Other bids** = 8+ HCP, natural

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

1. **Limited major openings** - Responder immediately knows 11-15 HCP
2. **Precision in game decisions** - Narrow ranges reduce guessing
3. **Efficient slam bidding** - Asking bids locate key cards
4. **Better preempts** - 2-level openings are not needed for strength
5. **Competitive edge** - Opponents must defend against unfamiliar structure

### Disadvantages

1. **1♣ is artificial** - Subject to interference (Mathe, etc.)
2. **1♦ is catch-all** - Can be very short (2 cards)
3. **Learning curve** - Complex relay structures
4. **Alerting** - Many bids require explanation
5. **Memory work** - Asking bid responses are step-based
