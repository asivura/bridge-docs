## System Families

### Natural Systems (4 or 5-Card Majors)

**Standard American / SAYC:**
- 5-card majors, 15-17 NT
- Most common worldwide, especially online
- See: [Our System](../SYSTEM.md)

**2/1 Game Forcing:**
- 5-card majors, 15-17 NT
- 2-level new suit response = game force
- Dominant in US tournaments
- See: [2/1 GF](TWO-OVER-ONE.md)

**Acol:**
- 4-card majors, **12-14 NT** (weak)
- Emphasizes limit bids
- Standard in UK and Commonwealth
- See: [Acol](ACOL.md)

**French Standard (SEF):**
- 5-card majors, 15-17 NT
- Similar to Standard American
- Official teaching system in France

### Strong Club Systems

**Precision Club:**
- 1♣ = 16+ HCP (artificial)
- Limited 1♥/1♠ (11-15)
- Popular in Asia
- See: [Precision](PRECISION.md)

**Polish Club:**
- 1♣ = various (12-14 balanced, 15+ with clubs, 18+ any)
- 15-17 NT
- Popular in Poland and Eastern Europe

**Blue Team Club:**
- 1♣ = 17+ HCP
- 4-card majors
- Historic Italian system

## Which System to Choose?

### For Beginners

**Recommendation: Standard American (SAYC)**
- Simple and natural
- Most partners will know it
- Works well online

### For Serious Tournament Play

**USA:** 2/1 Game Forcing
- Standard in ACBL events
- Better slam bidding

**UK/Australia:** Acol
- Expected at local clubs
- Add modern conventions as needed

### For Advanced Players

**Precision Club:**
- More precise bidding
- Better competitive weapons
- Requires dedicated partnership
