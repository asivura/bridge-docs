The most popular bidding system in modern American tournament bridge, based on Standard American with enhanced game-forcing mechanisms.

## System Overview

| Feature | 2/1 Specification |
|---------|-------------------|
| NT Range | **15-17** |
| Major Requirement | **5 cards** |
| 2-Level Response | **Game Forcing** |
| 1NT Response | Forcing (to major) |
| Strong Opening | 2♣ (22+ or 9+ tricks) |
| Origin | United States |
| Popularity | USA, Online (BBO) |

### Core Principle

A non-jump 2-level response to a 1-level opening is **unconditionally game-forcing**. This frees up bidding space for exploration and eliminates ambiguity.

## The 2/1 Game Force Principle

### When It Applies

A 2-level response in a new suit is GAME FORCING when:
- Partner opens **1♦, 1♥, or 1♠** in **1st or 2nd seat**
- Opponent **passes**
- Response is a **non-jump new suit** at 2-level

### 2/1 Responses

| Opening | 2/1 Responses | Requirements |
|---------|---------------|--------------|
| 1♦ | 2♣ | 12+ HCP, 4+ clubs |
| 1♥ | 2♣, 2♦ | 12+ HCP, 4+ cards in minor |
| 1♥ | - | (2♥ is a raise, not 2/1) |
| 1♠ | 2♣, 2♦, 2♥ | 12+ HCP, 5+ hearts for 2♥ |

### Key Points

- **2♥ over 1♠** promises 5+ hearts (game-forcing)
- Minor suit responses may be only 4 cards
- The auction **must** reach game (no stopping below)
- Responder's 12+ HCP means "opening hand values"

## Forcing 1NT Response

When partner opens **1♥ or 1♠**, a 1NT response is **forcing for one round**.

### Purpose

The Forcing 1NT handles hands that:
- Have 6-12 HCP without game-forcing values
- Lack support or a suit to bid at 2-level
- Need more information from opener

### 1NT Forcing Shows

| HCP | Support | Meaning |
|-----|---------|---------|
| 6-12 | 0-2 cards | No fit, forcing one round |
| 6-9 | 3 cards | Concealing support |
| 10-12 | 3 cards | Planning to invite |

### Opener's Rebids After 1NT Forcing

| Rebid | HCP | Meaning |
|-------|-----|---------|
| 2♣/2♦ | 12-18 | 3+ cards in minor (may be 3-card) |
| 2♥ (after 1♠) | 12-18 | 4+♥ |
| 2♥/2♠ (rebid) | 12-15 | 6+ card suit, minimum |
| 2NT | 18-19 | Balanced maximum |
| 3♣/3♦ | 16-18 | 5+ cards, good suit |
| 3♥/3♠ (rebid) | 16-18 | 6+ good suit, invitational |

### When 1NT is NOT Forcing

- Over 1♣ or 1♦ opening: 1NT = 6-10, not forcing
- By a passed hand
- After opponent's bid

## Responses to 1NT Opening

Opening 1NT shows **15-17 HCP, balanced**.

### Basic Responses

| Response | HCP | Meaning |
|----------|-----|---------|
| PASS | 0-7 | No game interest |
| 2♣(!) | 8+ | **Stayman** |
| 2♦(!) | 0+ | **Jacoby Transfer** to ♥ |
| 2♥(!) | 0+ | **Jacoby Transfer** to ♠ |
| 2♠(!) | 10+ | **Minor Suit Stayman** or transfer to ♣ |
| 2NT | 8-9 | Invitational, balanced |
| 3♣(!) | 10+ | **Puppet Stayman** or minor slam try |
| 3♦/3♥(!) | 10+ | Minor suit transfer (optional) |
| 3NT | 10-15 | To play |
| 4♣(!) | - | **Gerber** (asking for aces) |
| 4♦(!) | - | **Texas Transfer** to ♥ |
| 4♥(!) | - | **Texas Transfer** to ♠ |
| 4NT | 16-17 | Quantitative slam invite |

### Stayman Sequences

**1NT - 2♣ - 2♦ (no major):**

| Responder's Rebid | HCP | Meaning |
|-------------------|-----|---------|
| 2♥/2♠ | 8-9 | 5-card suit, invitational |
| 2NT | 8-9 | Invitational, no major |
| 3♣/3♦ | 10+ | 5+ cards, game forcing |
| 3NT | 10-15 | To play |

### After Jacoby Transfer

**1NT - 2♦ - 2♥:**

| Responder's Rebid | HCP | Meaning |
|-------------------|-----|---------|
| PASS | 0-7 | Weak, to play |
| 2♠ | 8-9 | 5♥-4♠, invitational |
| 2NT | 8-9 | 5♥, invitational |
| 3♣/3♦ | 10+ | Second suit, game forcing |
| 3♥ | 8-9 | 6+♥, invitational |
| 3NT | 10-15 | 5♥, choice of games |
| 4♥ | 10+ | 6+♥, to play |

## Major Suit Raises

### Direct Raises of 1♥/1♠

| Response | HCP | Trumps | Meaning |
|----------|-----|--------|---------|
| 2♥/2♠ | 5-10 | 3+ | Simple raise |
| 3♥/3♠ | 10-12 | 3+ | **Limit Raise** |
| 4♥/4♠ | 5-10 | 5+ | Weak, distributional |

### Jacoby 2NT

**Response of 2NT to 1♥/1♠** = Forcing raise with 4+ trumps and 13+ support points.

**Opener's Rebids:**

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 3♣/3♦/3♥ | Singleton or void in bid suit |
| 3♠ (over 1♥) | Singleton or void in ♠ |
| 3NT | 18-19 HCP, no shortness |
| 4 of trump | 12-14 HCP, no shortness |
| 3 of trump | 15-17 HCP, no shortness |

### Splinter Bids

A **double jump** in a new suit shows:
- 4+ trump support
- Singleton or void in bid suit
- 13-16 support points

**Examples:**
- 1♥ - 4♣/4♦ = splinter
- 1♠ - 4♣/4♦/4♥ = splinter

### Bergen Raises (Optional)

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 3♣ | 4+ trumps, 7-9 points |
| 3♦ | 4+ trumps, 10-12 points |
| 3♥/3♠ | 4+ trumps, 0-6 points (preemptive) |

## Opener's Rebids

### After a 2/1 Game Force

Both hands know game is reached; focus on strain and slam.

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 2 of own suit | 6+ cards, any strength |
| 2NT | 12-14 balanced, stoppers |
| 3NT | 15-17 balanced, stoppers |
| New suit | Natural, 4+ cards |
| Raise of responder | 3+ card support |
| Jump in new suit | Splinter (shortness) |

### After 1NT Forcing

See [Forcing 1NT Response](#forcing-1nt-response) above.

### After Limit Raise

| Opener's HCP | Action |
|--------------|--------|
| 12-14 | Pass (minimum) |
| 15-17 | Bid game |
| 18+ | Explore slam |

## Key Conventions

### Stayman

- **Bid:** 2♣ over 1NT/2NT
- **Purpose:** Find 4-4 major fit

### Jacoby Transfers

- **2♦ over 1NT:** Transfer to ♥
- **2♥ over 1NT:** Transfer to ♠

### Jacoby 2NT

- **2NT over 1♥/1♠:** Forcing raise, 4+ trumps, 13+

### Fourth Suit Forcing

Bidding the fourth suit is **artificial and game-forcing**, asking partner to describe hand further.

### New Minor Forcing

After 1m - 1M - 1NT, a bid of the **other minor** is artificial and forcing.

### Blackwood/RKCB

- **4NT:** Roman Key Card Blackwood
- **Responses (1430):** 5♣=1/4, 5♦=0/3, 5♥=2 without Q, 5♠=2 with Q

## When 2/1 Does NOT Apply

The 2/1 game force is **OFF** in these situations:

| Situation | Meaning of 2-Level Bid |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Opener in 3rd/4th seat | Not forcing to game |
| Responder is passed hand | 10+ but not forcing to game |
| Opponent overcalls | Natural, forcing one round |
| Opponent doubles | Natural, forcing one round |
| Over 1♣ opening | 2♦ = 10+, forcing one round |
