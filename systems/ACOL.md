# Acol Bidding System

The standard bidding system in British tournament play, named after the Acol Bridge Club in London where it evolved in the 1930s.

## System Overview

| Feature | Acol Specification |
|---------|-------------------|
| NT Range | **12-14 (Weak NT)** |
| Major Requirement | **4 cards** |
| Strong Opening | 2♣ (23+ or game-forcing) |
| Weak Twos | 2♦/♥/♠ (6-10, 6-card) |
| Origin | United Kingdom |
| Popularity | UK, Australia, New Zealand |

### Key Principles

1. **Limit Bids** - The most distinctive feature; bids closely define hand strength
2. **Four-Card Majors** - Only 4 cards required to open 1♥/1♠
3. **Weak No Trump** - 1NT shows 12-14 HCP balanced
4. **Natural Bidding** - All opening bids promise 4+ cards in the suit

## Opening Bids

| Open | HCP | Hand Description |
|------|-----|------------------|
| 1♣ | 12-19 | 4+♣, natural |
| 1♦ | 12-19 | 4+♦, natural |
| 1♥ | 12-19 | 4+♥ |
| 1♠ | 12-19 | 4+♠ |
| 1NT | **12-14** | Balanced (4-3-3-3, 4-4-3-2, 5-3-3-2) |
| 2♣ | 23+ | Strong artificial, game-forcing |
| 2♦ | 6-10 | 6+♦, weak two |
| 2♥ | 6-10 | 6+♥, weak two |
| 2♠ | 6-10 | 6+♠, weak two |
| 2NT | 20-22 | Balanced |
| 3X | 6-10 | 7+ card suit, preemptive |
| 3NT | 25-27 | Balanced |

### Suit Selection Rules

**With equal length suits:**
- **5-5:** Bid higher suit first ("high fives")
- **4-4:** Bid lower suit first
- **4-4 majors:** Open 1♥

**With 4-4-4-1 shape:**
- Singleton ♥ or ♠: Open 1♦
- Singleton ♦: Open 1♣
- Singleton ♣: Open 1♥

## Responses to 1NT

Opening 1NT shows **12-14 HCP, balanced**.

### Basic Responses

| Response | HCP | Meaning |
|----------|-----|---------|
| PASS | 0-10 | No game interest |
| 2♣(!) | 11+ | **Stayman** - asking for 4-card major |
| 2♦(!) | ANY | Transfer to ♥ (if playing transfers) |
| 2♥(!) | ANY | Transfer to ♠ (if playing transfers) |
| 2♥/2♠ | 0-10 | Weak takeout, 5+ cards (non-transfer) |
| 2NT | 11-12 | Invitational, balanced |
| 3♣/3♦ | 11-12 | Invitational, 5+ card suit |
| 3♥/3♠ | 10-12 | Invitational, 5+ card suit |
| 3NT | 13-18 | To play |
| 4♥/4♠ | 13+ | To play, 6+ card suit |
| 4NT | 19-20 | Quantitative slam invite |

### After Stayman (1NT - 2♣)

| Opener's Rebid | Meaning |
|----------------|---------|
| 2♦ | No 4-card major |
| 2♥ | 4+♥ (may have 4♠) |
| 2♠ | 4♠, denies 4♥ |

### Opener's Action on 2NT Invite

| Opener's HCP | Action |
|--------------|--------|
| 12 | Pass |
| 13 | Bid 3NT with extras (tens, good shape) |
| 14 | Bid 3NT |

## Responses to Suit Openings

Opening 1♣/1♦/1♥/1♠ shows **12-19 HCP, 4+ cards**.

### Response Strength Guide

| Points | Action |
|--------|--------|
| 0-5 | Pass (usually) |
| 6-9 | Simple response (one-level or single raise) |
| 10-12 | Invitational (jump raise or 2NT) |
| 13-15 | Game forcing (new suit at 2-level, then bid game) |
| 16+ | Slam interest |

### Responses to 1♥/1♠ Opening

| Response | HCP | Meaning |
|----------|-----|---------|
| 1♠ (over 1♥) | 6+ | 4+♠, forcing one round |
| 1NT | 6-9 | No fit, no biddable suit at 1-level |
| 2♣/2♦ | 10+ | 4+ cards, forcing one round |
| 2♥/2♠ (raise) | 6-9 | 4+ trump support |
| 2NT | 10-12 | Balanced, invitational |
| 3♥/3♠ (jump raise) | 10-12 | **Limit raise**, 4+ trumps |
| 3NT | 13-15 | Balanced, to play |
| 4♥/4♠ | 6-10 | 5+ trumps, distributional |

### Responses to 1♣/1♦ Opening

| Response | HCP | Meaning |
|----------|-----|---------|
| 1♦/1♥/1♠ | 6+ | 4+ cards, forcing one round |
| 1NT | 6-9 | No 4-card major |
| 2♣ (over 1♦) | 10+ | 4+♣, forcing |
| 2♦/3♣ (raise) | 6-9 | Support |
| 2NT | 10-12 | Balanced, invitational |
| 3♦/4♣ (jump raise) | 10-12 | Limit raise |
| 3NT | 13-15 | Balanced, to play |

## Strong Two Openings

### 2♣ Opening (Artificial, Game-Forcing)

Shows **23+ HCP** or a hand with 9+ playing tricks.

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 2♦(!) | Waiting/negative (0-7 HCP) |
| 2♥/2♠ | 8+ HCP, good 5+ card suit |
| 2NT | 8+ HCP, balanced |
| 3♣/3♦ | 8+ HCP, good 5+ card suit |

### Opener's Rebids After 2♣ - 2♦

| Rebid | HCP | Meaning |
|-------|-----|---------|
| 2♥/2♠ | 23+ | 5+ card suit, forcing |
| 2NT | 23-24 | Balanced |
| 3♣/3♦ | 23+ | 5+ card suit, forcing |
| 3NT | 25-27 | Balanced |

## Weak Two Openings

2♦/2♥/2♠ show **6-10 HCP** with a **6-card suit**.

### Responses to Weak Twos

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| PASS | Weak, no fit |
| 2NT(!) | Asking bid (Ogust or Feature) |
| Raise | Preemptive or to play |
| New suit | Forcing (one round) |
| 3NT | To play |
| 4♥/4♠ | To play |

### Ogust Convention (After 2NT Ask)

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 3♣ | Minimum, bad suit |
| 3♦ | Minimum, good suit |
| 3♥ | Maximum, bad suit |
| 3♠ | Maximum, good suit |
| 3NT | AKQxxx in suit |

## Key Conventions

### Stayman

- **Bid:** 2♣ over 1NT
- **Purpose:** Find 4-4 major fit
- **Responses:** 2♦ (no major), 2♥ (4+ hearts), 2♠ (4 spades)

### Transfers (Optional)

Many Acol pairs use Jacoby Transfers:
- **2♦:** Transfer to ♥
- **2♥:** Transfer to ♠

### Blackwood (4NT)

Asking for aces:
- **5♣:** 0 or 4 aces
- **5♦:** 1 ace
- **5♥:** 2 aces
- **5♠:** 3 aces

### Fourth Suit Forcing

A bid of the fourth suit is artificial and forcing, asking opener to describe hand further.

### Trial Bids

After a major suit raise, a new suit asks about help in that suit for game decision.

## Comparison with Standard American

| Feature | Acol | Standard American |
|---------|------|-------------------|
| 1NT Range | **12-14** | 15-17 |
| Major Requirement | **4 cards** | 5 cards |
| With 15-17 balanced | Open 1-suit | Open 1NT |
| With 12-14 balanced | Open 1NT | Open 1-suit |
| Weak Twos | 2♦/♥/♠ | 2♦/♥/♠ |
| Limit Raises | Direct jump | May use conventions |
| Common in | UK, Commonwealth | USA, Online |

### Key Differences in Practice

1. **Opening Lead:** With 12-14 balanced, Acol opens 1NT; SAYC opens 1-suit
2. **Major Suit Opening:** Acol can open 1♥/1♠ with 4 cards
3. **Rebid Structure:** Different because of NT ranges
4. **Limit Bids:** Acol emphasizes precise limit bidding
